Core concept is clear now:
- Scorecard marketing is a lead-generation system, not just a quiz UI.
- Goal is to generate warm, data-rich leads before sales calls by giving users a fast personalized diagnosis.
- It works because people want to score/rank/improve themselves, and good scorecards surface their hidden tension.
The book’s practical framework is 4 steps:
1. Landing page  
   - One job: get people to start the quiz.  
   - Must deliver: clarity (what they get), credibility (why trust you), connection (this is for me), strong CTA.
2. Questionnaire  
   - Fast, simple, engaging (typically yes/no or easy choices).  
   - Organized in categories, with points.  
   - Should reveal motivation, blockers, readiness.
3. Results  
   - Show overall + category scores.  
   - Use tiers and dynamic/personalized messaging.  
   - Give a clear next step per tier (offer/content/call).
4. Marketing + sales follow-up  
   - Promote scorecard as main CTA across channels.  
   - Follow up quickly using the lead’s score data.  
   - Use data to segment and improve conversion over time.
Most important strategic ideas:
- A scorecard is a product-for-prospects (low-friction first step).
- Personalization is the advantage: collect more than email; use data in follow-up.
- Keep it simple and conversion-focused; iterate by conversion results.
