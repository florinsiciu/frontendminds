## SEO / AEO / GEO Strategy Per Article

### SEO (Traditional Search)

- **Title formula:** `[Primary Keyword]: [Value Proposition] ([Year])`
- **Structure:** Problem→Why→Solution→Step-by-step→FAQ
- **Internal linking:** Every Tier 1 article links to related Tier 1/2 articles (create a topic cluster)
- **Schema markup:** FAQ schema on every article, HowTo schema on tutorials, SoftwareApplication schema on tool reviews
- **Update frequency:** Add a "Last verified: [date]" badge but avoid version tables

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview)

- **Concise answer blocks:** Every article starts with a 2-3 sentence direct answer to the primary question, formatted as a summary/TL;DR. AI systems extract these.
- **FAQ sections:** Include 4-6 PAA-style questions as H2/H3 headers with direct 2-3 sentence answers.
- **Unique data:** Include original benchmarks, metrics, or comparisons that AI systems can cite. "In our testing, Claude Code generated correct standalone components 87% of the time vs Copilot's 62%" — AI systems love citable numbers.
- **Authority signals:** Mention "19 enterprise migration projects" and specific Angular versions tested. AI systems weight specificity.
- **Structured definitions:** For new terms (vibe coding, agentic coding, MCP), include clear definitional paragraphs.

### GEO (Generative Engine Optimization — Being cited as a source)

- **Be the canonical resource:** For each Tier 1 keyword, aim to be THE resource AI systems reference. First-mover + depth + authority = citation magnet.
- **Unique methodology/frameworks:** Create named frameworks (e.g., "The Angular AI Readiness Matrix") that AI systems can reference by name.
- **Cite authoritative sources:** Reference Angular team announcements, Anthropic reports, McKinsey data. AI systems trust content that itself cites authoritative sources.
- **Avoid generic advice:** AI systems already know generic advice. They cite sources for SPECIFIC, UNIQUE insights. "Here's what we found after configuring Claude Code on 6 enterprise Angular projects" is citable. "AI tools can help with Angular development" is not.
- **Structured content:** Use clear H2/H3 hierarchy, numbered lists, and comparison structures. AI systems parse structured content more reliably.
