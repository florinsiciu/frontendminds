Yes — there’s a real trend there, but people often mash together **three different business models** and call all of them “making money from Google without SEO.”

They are not the same.

## 1. **Rank-and-Rent** (probably what you saw with backlinks + local businesses)

This is the classic.

### How it works

You build a simple website like:

- “Emergency Plumber in Phoenix”
    
- “Tree Removal Dallas”
    
- “Kitchen Remodeling Austin”
    

You get **Google traffic** (often with lighter SEO + backlinks + Google Business Profile tricks), generate leads, then:

- Rent the site to a business for monthly fee
    
- Sell leads per call/form
    
- Forward calls to contractors
    

### Model:

Traffic → leads → sell leads

Example:

- 30 calls/month
    
- Sell each lead for $50
    
- = $1,500/month from one site
    

Or rent the whole site:

- $750–$3,000/month/site
    

### Why people like it

- One asset can pay monthly
    
- Businesses care about leads, not websites
    
- Can scale by city/service
    

### Why “backlinks” matter

Because many of these people still do:

- local citations
    
- directory links
    
- niche edits
    
- expired domains
    
- parasite pages
    
- Google Maps optimization
    

This is probably what you're seeing.

### Best niches

High-ticket emergency services:

- Roofing
    
- Water damage
    
- HVAC
    
- Personal injury leads
    
- Junk removal
    
- Pest control
    
- Med spas
    

(avoid restaurants; low margins)

Very hot right now.

This may be the “without ranking” angle you heard.

You don’t rank your own site.

- Reddit threads
    
- YouTube videos
    
- Pinterest pins
    

Use them to capture search traffic and funnel to:

- affiliate offers
    
- digital products
    
- local leads
    

People love calling this “free Google traffic.”

Really it’s rented attention.
