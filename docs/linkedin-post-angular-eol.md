# LinkedIn Post

I've led Angular upgrades across 19 enterprise applications. The pattern I keep seeing: teams treat version upgrades as "someday" projects, then get hit with a compliance emergency when their version goes EOL.

Angular 19 hit end-of-life on May 19. Angular 22 launched June 3. That means teams on v19 are now TWO versions behind with zero security patches.

Three things I tell every CTO who asks "how bad is it?":

1. Check your version: run "ng version" right now
2. If you're on v19 or older, you have unpatched security vulnerabilities
3. Every version jump takes 1-2 weeks for enterprise apps — and you can't skip versions

The 18-month support window moves fast. Angular 16 felt current when it launched in May 2023. By November 2024 — just 18 months later — it was end-of-life.

I put together a full EOL status table and upgrade guide covering every version from 14 to 22, with realistic timelines from actual enterprise projects: https://frontendminds.com/blog/angular-end-of-life-2026
